Competency Goal I

To Establish and Maintain Safe Healthy Environment

I provide a safe environment and teach children safe practices to prevent and reduce injuries by setting the room free of rolling toys that may cause injuries to both children and adults/teachers by stepping on those and tripping. Babies at age below 18 months donâ€™t really know how to put the toy back after playing with it and so they leave them as is. It is our responsibility as adults to pick those up after them, especially when they play with the stacking toys. Stacking toys are those with varying sizes and the smaller the toy gets, the more hazardous they become.

I provide an environment that promotes health and prevents illness by picking up the toys they played with and securing them in dirty toys bin securely. I sanitize the toys they played with soap and bleach solution at the end of the day or when time permits. Sanitizing toys every day is the most important task for the day as most of the babies put the toys in their mouth as that helps them with their teeth. By sanitizing those toys at the end of day, it helps them from getting sick by using them the next day. Not only the toys they played with, I also sanitize the surfaces they played on, the glass doors and windows they touch, the sheets they nap on and the high chairs they sit on to eat.

I organize the physical spaces in such a way that they are secure, safe and at approachable distance to the babies when crisis arises. Areas such as dropping zone, play area, reading area, dining area, changing area and napping area separated from each other and at the same time not very distant from each other. I organize my weekly planned materials, daily schedules and routines in such a way that they are interesting and enjoyable and that promotes engagement, play, exploration and learning for all the babies.

My room design is appropriate for optimum learning. I set up spaces with clear labels with pictures of how they are supposed to look. I make sure I have lots of materials so that I can change them out often to keep the children interested in learning. I provide age-appropriate tables and chairs. I have a wonderful library center that has soft comfortable seating with interesting books. I provide other centers such as home centers that help children learn social and communication skills, a play center with structure to keep them active and focused all the time and a writing center to scribble on their ideas. I change out the materials in these centers every month to encourage and excel the greatest growth and development for my young children.

My lesson plans reflect my organizational skills. I am dedicated to teaching interesting and age-appropriate materials. Repeating words, a concept or a skill allows the children to form an understanding and even try to imitate it. Children may repeat unfamiliar words back to me as they learn them, and they will learn letters and words by repeatedly seeing them written down. Repetition helps us remember embedded information in our long-term memory. Hence, I use my themes to ensure repetition in whatever I am teaching. I repeat a few of the popular activities on a weekly basis which help them learn from those. Our school lets children participate in holiday activities such as Easter, Halloween, Thanksgiving, Christmas, Diwali etc., so they do not lose their holiday spirit.

Our school lets the parents provide us with extensive information on the way their baby eats. They provide us with the formula/milk, information on the amounts they eat and the times they eat at. If the baby likes or dislikes the food, we let the parents know so they are aware of what their baby ate for the day and plan accordingly for the next day.
